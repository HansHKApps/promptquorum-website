What Is Temperature in AI?

Temperature is a knob that controls randomness.

  • At 0.0: Always picks the most likely word → Identical output every time
  • At 1.0+: Considers riskier alternatives → Surprising & varied text

What Is Top-P (Nucleus Sampling)?

Top-P controls how many word options the model considers at each step.

  • At 0.1: Narrow & safe — only very top options
  • At 0.9: Looser & more varied — wider set of choices

Temperature vs Top-P: Key Differences

Temperature = "How adventurous"

Top-P = "How many options to consider"

💡 Pro tip: Tune ONE, keep the other at default

Temperature Ranges at a Glance

Range Behavior Best For
Low (0.0–0.3) Focused, repetitive, stable Code, data extraction
Medium (0.4–0.7) Balanced variation & stability Most general tasks
High (0.8–1.0+) Creative, diverse, surprising Brainstorming, storytelling

Recommended Settings by Use Case

Use Case Temp Top-P
Coding/Refactoring 0.1–0.3 0.95
Summaries & Explanations 0.4–0.6 0.9
Brainstorming & Creative 0.7–1.0 1.0
Data Extraction 0.0–0.2 0.95
Long-form Writing 0.6–0.8 0.9–1.0

Quick-Start Recipes

  • Safe Factual: T=0.2, Top-P=0.95 → Reliable, minimal hallucination
  • Balanced Default: T=0.5, Top-P=0.9 → Natural, stable
  • Creative Brainstorming: T=0.8, Top-P=1.0 → Diverse, surprising
  • Short-Answer: T=0.3, Top-P=0.95 → Fast, direct
  • Experimental: T=1.0, Top-P=1.0 → Unpredictable, max variation

Prompts Matter More Than Settings

Same Prompt at Different Temperatures:

Write a short product tagline for a productivity app. Under 10 words.

At T=0.2: "Get more done in less time."

At T=0.8: "Chaos to clarity: where moments transform into momentum."

When High Temperature Becomes Risky

Be conservative (T=0.0–0.5) for:

  • Code that goes to production
  • Health & medical advice
  • Finance & legal analysis
  • Safety-critical decisions

7 Common Mistakes

  1. Cranking both to max and expecting reliability
  2. Changing both knobs at once (can't isolate what helped)
  3. Trying to fix a bad prompt with sliders
  4. Forgetting models interpret values differently
  5. Testing only one output per setting
  6. Setting T=0 and expecting perfect correctness
  7. Ignoring top-p entirely because your provider ignores it

5 Steps to Set Temperature Right

  1. Fix the prompt first. Write clear instructions.
  2. Identify task type. Factual (0.0–0.3) or Creative (0.7–1.0)?
  3. Leave top-p at default (0.9–1.0). Only adjust if you have a specific reason.
  4. Run 3–5 test prompts at your target temperature.
  5. Document your settings per use case.

Key FAQs

Q: Adjust temperature or top-p first?
A: Temperature. Keep top-p at default (0.9–1.0) until you understand temperature.

Q: Can T=0 guarantee correctness?
A: No. Hallucinations come from training gaps, not random sampling. Lower temperature makes outputs consistent—but not always correct.
